Biography
A stitched narrative: migration → curiosity → building → art

Abhay Shevyam is a computer science major at the University of Washington. Born in India, he moved to the United States at age two and grew up in Seattle.

His identity is shaped by living across multiple geographies— Paris, Ireland, and Stockholm—before settling into a long-term home base in Seattle. In ninth grade, he discovered a sustained interest in coding and entrepreneurship.

Early spark

Abhay describes coding as both tool and language: a way to prototype futures quickly. That instinct later expanded into building products and companies—first as a student, then through an early startup called Professor AI.

Parallel practices

Outside software, Abhay maintains a consistent visual practice in oil painting, and a reflective practice through philosophy and film analysis. He also studies human health and anatomy—part curiosity, part lived experience through injury recovery—and places his finances into investing.

Alternative futures (hypertext)

This project treats each interest as a door. Depending on which door you click, “Abhay” becomes:

  • a founder scaling an app studio
  • a painter cataloguing a private archive
  • a reader building a personal philosophy library
  • an anatomy hobbyist turning curiosity into a health layer
